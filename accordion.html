FAQ

➤ Why do our fingers prune when taking a long bath?

It is not because your skin is bsorbing water like a sponge. Scientist discovered that this wrinkling is actually an involuntary reaction controlled by the nervous system. The brain causes blood vessels just below the skin to constrict, likely to give us a better grip on wet objects -- acting like a built-in "tread" for your fingertips.

➤ Does the Earth's rotation ever change?

Yes. The Earth's rotation is gradually slowing down. As a result, the average length of a day increases by about 1.8 seconds every century. For example, 600 million years ago, a single day lasted just 21 hours.

➤ Why are our stomachs able to digest themselves?

The stomach contains powerful hydrochloric acid used to break down food, but it is lined with a thick layer of protective mucus. Additionally, the stomach continuously produces and secretes bicarbonate, which neutralizes the acid right against the stomach lining, preventing it from digesting its own tissue.

➤ Does outer space have a smell?

Yes. According to astronauts, space surprisingly has a distinct, harsh smell. It is described as a mix of hot metal, seared steak, welding fumes, and gunpowder, likely caused by volatile compounds floating around after dying stars and chemical reactions.

➤ Is it true a day on Venus is longer than a year on Venus?

Yes! Venus has an incredibly slow axis rotation. It takes Venus 243 Earth days to rotate once on its axis, but only 225 Earth days to complete one orbit around the Sun.
